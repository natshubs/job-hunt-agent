# Career-Ops Filtering Rules

Claude should filter broadly at first, then prioritize.

Do not make the search too narrow. The scraper may return hundreds of jobs, and Claude should sort them after scraping.

## Keep / Review Jobs

Keep jobs for review if they involve any of the following:

- Medical affairs
- Medical strategy
- Clinical development
- Clinical science
- Clinical research strategy
- Medical communications with strategic or scientific ownership
- Medical education
- Medical science liaison
- Field medical
- Clinical product strategy
- Healthcare AI
- Digital health
- Clinical AI
- Genomics
- Precision medicine
- Biomarkers
- Psychiatry
- Neuroscience
- CNS
- Neuropsychiatry
- Oncology, if the role uses medical affairs, clinical development, biomarkers, AI, or strategy
- Cardiology, if the role uses clinical development, medical affairs, trials, medical devices, or strategy
- Life sciences consulting
- Evidence generation
- Real-world evidence
- HEOR, if clinically strategic and not purely statistical
- Medical device clinical strategy
- Clinical operations only if it includes strategy, trial design, medical monitoring, or cross-functional leadership

## Strong Positive Signals

Increase priority for jobs mentioning:

- MD, DO, PharmD, PhD, advanced clinical degree
- Medical affairs
- Clinical development
- KOL engagement
- Scientific exchange
- Evidence generation
- Medical strategy
- Launch strategy
- Medical education
- Clinical trial design
- Protocol development
- AI / machine learning in healthcare
- LLM evaluation
- Genomics / precision medicine
- Psychiatry / neuroscience / CNS / neuropsychiatry
- Cross-functional leadership
- Regulatory collaboration
- FDA
- Real-world evidence
- Product strategy
- Digital health
- Patient outcomes
- Scientific communications

## Exclude Jobs

Exclude jobs that are clearly:

- Chiropractor
- Physical therapist
- Nurse-only
- RN, NP, PA-only unless the role also accepts MD and is strategic
- Dentist
- Veterinarian
- Pharmacist-only retail role
- Medical assistant
- Clinical research coordinator
- Entry-level lab technician
- Pure regulatory affairs
- Pure quality assurance
- Pure medical writing
- Pure health informatics
- Pure data entry
- Pure billing/coding
- Pure sales / account executive
- Pharmaceutical sales representative
- Device sales representative
- Territory sales manager
- Roles requiring an active unrestricted board certification if Natasha does not meet it
- Roles requiring completed residency if the role absolutely requires it
- Vice President / CMO / Head of Medical Affairs if clearly too senior
- Academic faculty roles requiring board certification or completed residency
- Hospitalist / attending physician roles requiring board certification or full residency completion

## Gray Area Jobs

Mark as “Needs Human Review” rather than excluding if:

- It is a medical affairs role but therapeutic area is not psychiatry.
- It is oncology/cardiology but strategic and aligned with Natasha’s prior experience.
- It is AI/healthcare but not clearly clinical.
- It is product management but healthcare/clinical relevance is unclear.
- It is consulting but seniority is unclear.
- It mentions MD preferred but not required.
- It is MSL outside psychiatry but close enough, such as neurology, CNS, rare disease, genomics, or pain.

## Scoring

Score each job from 1–5.

5 = Excellent fit. Strongly aligned with Natasha’s profile and worth applying.
4 = Good fit. Likely worth applying.
3 = Possible fit. Needs human review.
2 = Weak fit. Probably skip unless there is a special reason.
1 = Exclude.
